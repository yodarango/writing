There is a law in thermodynamics that states that all things tend toward a state of deterioration. It seems to me that this law applies not only to the physical world but also to the world of abstracts. For example, words, which once had a very intense meaning, with frequent use over time lose this intensity. The word 'terrible,' for instance, in the past represented something particularly frightening. But today, we find it used in everyday conversations. Thus, the cycle repeats itself, creating the need for new words.
