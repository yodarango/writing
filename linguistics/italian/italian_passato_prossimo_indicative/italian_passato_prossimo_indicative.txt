    Introduction
    
      The Passato Prossimo is used for actions that happened at a specific time
      in the recent past and have been completed. It is composed of two things,
      a helping verb and a participle verb. The helping verbs are
      essere and avere</i
      >. If the verb uses the auxiliary essere</i
      >, then the participle must agree in number and gender
    

    Conjugation of the Participle Past
    
      
        -are
        
          singular: ato
          plural: ati
        
      
      
        -ere
        
          singular: uto
          plural: uti
        
      
      
        -ire
        
          singular: ito
          plural: iti
        
      
    

    Irregularities

    
      Most verbs use the auxiliary verb avere, however, verbs that convey
      movement use the verb essere along with other few ones like
      the verb essere itself.
      <span class="bg-eta"
        >The Passato Prossimo is by far the most common tense of the past in
        Italian.</span
      >
      
    
    
      Examples:
      
        singular masculine: Lui è mangiato dal cane.
        singular feminine: Lei è vista dalla polizia.
        plural masculine: Loro sono partiti presto.
        plural feminine: Loro sono mangiate dalle formiche.
      
    

    When to Use
    
      It may be used in most situations where the English “I have done” or “I
      did” would satisfy the case.
    

    Examples
    
      Recent Actions:
      
        🇮🇹 Ho mangiato una mela.
        🇲🇽 He comido una manzana.
        🇺🇸 I have eaten an apple.
      
    

    
      Actions Completed at an Unspecified Time in the Past:
      
        🇮🇹 Abbiamo visto quel film molte volte.
        🇲🇽 Hemos visto esa película muchas veces.
        🇺🇸 We have watched that movie many times.
      
    

    
      Actions That Have Relevance to the Present:
      
        🇮🇹 Hanno finito i compiti.
        🇲🇽 Han terminado los deberes.
        🇺🇸 They have finished their homework.
