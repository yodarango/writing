Introduction

The presente condizionale makes for a long conjugation because it starts from the infinitive verb (omitting the ending E), rather than from the stem, as other verbs. Thus to form the conditional form of the verb mettere, we start from metter. Nonetheless, this is a relatively easy conjugation since all the regular verbs follow the same endings without exceptions. Therefore, the following endings apply to verbs ending in -are, -ere, -ire:

  • Singular: erei, esti, ebbe
  • Plural: emmo, este, ebbero

There is one important note about the verbs ending in -are. The ending A is exchanged for an E. Therefore, we would not start from pagar, but from pagher. Notice also the addition of the H in order to keep a consistent pronunciation. While talking about pronunciation, it must be kept in mind that verbs ending in -iare eradicate the I.

Example:

  • mangiare → mangerei

When to Use

It may be used in most situations where the English “I would …” or “I could…” satisfies the case or where the Spanish 1PS verb -ia satisfies the case.

Examples

Hypothetical Situations:

  • 🇮🇹 Comprerei una casa se avessi i soldi.
  • 🇲🇽 Compraría una casa si tuviera el dinero.
  • 🇺🇸 I would buy a house if I had the money.

Polite Requests:

  • 🇮🇹 Mi passeresti il sale, per favore?
  • 🇲🇽 ¿Me pasarías la sal, por favor?
  • 🇺🇸 Would you pass me the salt, please?

Expressing Wishes or Desires:

  • 🇮🇹 Vorrei andare in vacanza quest'estate.
  • 🇲🇽 Me gustaría ir de vacaciones este verano.
  • 🇺🇸 I would like to go on vacation this summer.

Giving Advice or Suggestions:

  • 🇮🇹 Dovresti studiare di più per l’esame.
  • 🇲🇽 Deberías estudiar más para el examen.
  • 🇺🇸 You should study more for the exam.
