Introduction

The passato imperfetto is used for ongoing or habitual actions in the past. It signifies that an action not only happened once, but it was an ongoing action. The imperfect past is second to the perfect past in terms of popularity and day-to-day usage, meaning that it is used extensively in prolonged conversations. A solid knowledge of the passato prossimo and the passato imperfetto will render the learner ready with most of the past conjugations needed to carry a meaningful conversation in Italian. This tense does not make use of a helping verb like the passato perfetto does. There are also fewer irregular cases.

Conjugation

  • -are

    • Singular: avo, avi, ava
    • Plural: avamo, avate, avano

  • -ere

    • Singular: evo, evi, eva
    • Plural: evamo, evate, evano

  • -ire

    • Singular: ivo, ivi, iva
    • Plural: ivamo, ivate, ivano
